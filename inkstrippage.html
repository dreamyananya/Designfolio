About:

Comic enthusiasts and aspiring artists face challenges in discovering rare comics, engaging with the community, and showcasing their art effectively. Our platform aims to bridge these gaps by providing comprehensive resources and a supportive network.



Problem Statement:

InkStrip addresses the challenges faced by both Jake, a passionate comic artist, and Lily, a devoted reader. Jake struggles with limited platforms offering the right tools and visibility to showcase his comics, while Lily finds it difficult to discover fresh, interactive content that aligns with her interests.



Project Background and Objective:

InkStrip is designed to help creators like Jake easily publish, distribute, and monetize comics, while also providing readers like Lily with a rich, interactive experience. By offering specialized tools, community engagement features, and a diverse comic library, InkStrip connects creators with their audience and enhances the reading experience.



Key Takings from the end-users before redesigning:

  • Jake (Comic Artist): "I can never seem to get my comics noticed on other platforms. The tools aren't really made for comic creators, and it feels like a hassle to format everything. I just want to focus on creating, not on dealing with technical issues."

  • Lily (Reader): "It’s hard to find comics that stand out. I’m always looking for fresh stories, but it feels like the platforms are either too cluttered or don’t offer enough customization to find what I enjoy. I want something more interactive and engaging."



Competitive Audit Findings:

  1. Wattpad:

    • Strength:

      • Large User Base, giving creators significant exposure and community interaction.
      • Clean and simple outlook.
    • Weakness:

      • Primarily a platform for prose, limiting tools and features specifically for comic creators.

  2. GlobalComix:

    • Strength:

      • Supports multiple languages and offers flexible monetization options like subscriptions and pay-per-chapter.
    • Weakness:
      • Has a more niche audience compared to Wattpad, which may limit visibility for new creators.
